accordingly.Build a survival kit.
It should include the following:
Jerry Cans
Clean drinking water.
The most import part of your survival, store 1 gallon of water for each person per day.
Keep it in jerry cans or water jugs and write "Drinking Water" on the side of these containers with a permanent marker.
Add four drops of Clorox per gallon to keep the water good for years.
MRE
Food.
Military-style MRE's (Meals Ready to Eat) are an excellent choice.
First Aid Kit
Complete first-aid kit along with any medications needed.
A variety of survival style knives.
A survival knife is the most important and useful tool in the kit.
Be sure to keep it sharp.
Mace Spray.
Self defense weapon.
Mace spray is a good economic choice, although a tazer or pistol works just as well.
Keep in mind that if you choose mace, it does expire and will need to be replaced occasionally.
Matches
Waterproof and windproof matches.
Flashlight.
Waterproof LED flashlight.
Small Radio
Battery or crank powered radio.
Whistle
Whistle for signaling for help.
Batteries
Batteries for any electronic equipment.
