problems/eventsKnow your subject.
Check the library or the internet for information so that you can speak truthfully and with depth.
Think about your messaging.
Outline the key points on paper.
Make sure your points are consistent with speeches you have given in the past, unless you are purposely contradicting yourself.
If you are writing a speech on, say, politics, don't just give the people what they want to hear.
Find a happy medium between what must be said and what will be palatable to your target audience.
Make it interesting by including understandable jokes, or captivating illustrations or stories.
Make your speech simple and to the point.
